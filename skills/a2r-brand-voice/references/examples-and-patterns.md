# A2R Brand Voice Examples and Patterns

> Source: Brand Voice Interview (2026), A2R Brandbook, and Notion brand materials.

This file provides concrete before/after examples, full content patterns, and anti-patterns to help apply A2R's brand voice correctly.

---

## Before/After Rewrites

### Rewrite 1: Website Hero Headline

**Before** (generic EdTech):
> "Empowering educators with cutting-edge AI solutions for the next generation of learning."

**Problems**: Uses "empowering" (banned), "cutting-edge" (banned buzzword), "solutions" (vague), "next generation" (banned). No rebel hook. No personality.

**After** (A2R voice):
> "Education hasn't evolved in decades. We've spent fifteen years proving it can."

**Why it works**: Rebel pillar opens with a bold challenge. Expert pillar grounds it immediately. Short, punchy Hemingway sentences. No buzzwords. Creates "inspired possibility."

---

### Rewrite 2: Product Description

**Before** (corporate):
> "Our end-to-end solution leverages advanced AI to deliver a holistic approach to personalized learning, empowering stakeholders to move the needle on student outcomes."

**Problems**: Five banned terms in one sentence ("end-to-end solution", "leverages", "holistic approach", "empowering", "move the needle"). "Stakeholders" instead of specific audiences. Passive, impersonal tone.

**After** (A2R voice):
> "Our AI adapts to each learner in real time. It listens, adjusts, and builds a path that fits — not a one-size-fits-all track. The result: learners who stay engaged and educators who see the difference."

**Why it works**: AI personified ("listens, adjusts, builds"). Short sentences. Concrete outcomes. Collective result framing. No buzzwords.

---

### Rewrite 3: Sales Proposal Opening

**Before** (generic pitch):
> "Dear Dr. Martinez, We are pleased to present our comprehensive adaptive learning platform, which provides a scalable solution for universities seeking to enhance student engagement and retention."

**Problems**: Stiff, corporate opening. "Pleased to present" (not A2R). "Comprehensive" and "scalable solution" (buzzwords). Doesn't reference the prospect's specific challenge.

**After** (A2R voice):
> "Dr. Martinez — your team shared that first-year retention is the priority this year. That challenge is exactly where we've seen the biggest impact. When we partnered with [University X], they moved from 60% to 84% completion in one semester. Here's how we'd approach it with you."

**Why it works**: Opens with the prospect's challenge. Evidence immediately (case narrative + stat). Collaborative framing ("with you"). Pragmatic and concrete — no poetic language. Direct.

---

### Rewrite 4: Social Media Post

**Before** (corporate social):
> "We are excited to announce our new partnership with [Publisher] to deliver best-in-class adaptive learning solutions. #EdTech #AI #Innovation #PersonalizedLearning"

**Problems**: "Excited to announce" (corporate formula). "Best-in-class" (banned). "Solutions" (vague). Hashtag stuffing.

**After** (A2R voice):
> "Every learner is different. So why does most content treat them the same? We just partnered with [Publisher] to change that. Adaptive learning that actually adapts. More soon."

**Why it works**: Opens with a provocative question (rebel pillar). Playful, conversational tone. AI personified through implication. Short. Invites curiosity ("More soon"). No hashtag stuffing.

---

### Rewrite 5: Bad News Communication

**Before** (corporate non-answer):
> "We take this matter very seriously and are working diligently to resolve the issue. We apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused and appreciate your patience."

**Problems**: "Take this seriously" (empty phrase). "Any inconvenience" (minimizes impact). "Appreciate your patience" (assumes patience). No concrete next step.

**After** (A2R voice):
> "We know the integration delay is affecting your launch timeline. That's not what we committed to, and we own that. Here's where we are: the root cause is identified and the fix is in testing. We expect to have this resolved by Thursday. We'll update you Wednesday regardless of status."

**Why it works**: Acknowledges specific impact. Takes ownership ("we own that"). Concrete status and timeline. Commits to proactive update. Collective "we" throughout. Calm, confident, direct.

---

### Rewrite 6: Spanish Localization

**English source**:
> "Education hasn't evolved in decades. We've spent fifteen years proving it can."

**Before** (literal translation):
> "La educación no ha evolucionado en décadas. Hemos pasado quince años demostrando que puede."

**Problems**: Technically correct but flat. "Hemos pasado" (spent/passed) is weak — doesn't convey the deliberate effort.

**After** (A2R punchy Spanish):
> "La educación no ha evolucionado en décadas. Hemos dedicado quince años a demostrar que sí puede."

**Why it works**: "Dedicado" (dedicated) carries more intentionality than "pasado". "Sí puede" (yes, it can) adds emphasis and rebel confidence. Slightly longer than English but still punchy for Spanish. The bold claim and expert grounding are both preserved.

## Full Content Examples

### Example A: Website Hero Section (Complete)

```
OVERLINE: ADAPTIVE LEARNING, REIMAGINED

HEADLINE: We don't replace teachers.
We give them superpowers.

BODY: For fifteen years, we've built AI that listens to each learner
and adapts in real time. Not a script. Not a template. A living,
breathing learning experience that grows with every student.

CTA: See what adaptive learning actually looks like.
```

**Voice checklist**: Rebel hook (headline) + expert grounding ("fifteen years") + AI personified ("listens", "adapts", "grows") + soundbite (headline) + provocative CTA + inspired possibility + no buzzwords.

### Example B: Blog Post Opening

> **Title**: The Myth of One-Size-Fits-All Learning
>
> Every year, millions of learners sit through the same content at the same pace — and we call it "personalized" because they chose it from a catalog.
>
> That's not personalization. That's a menu.

> We've been building this for fifteen years. Here's what we've learned.

**Voice checklist**: Rebel hook (provocative reframing) + layered depth (simple → specific) + AI personified ("listens", "adjusts") + expert grounding ("fifteen years") + Hemingway style (short sentences, short paragraphs) + no buzzwords.

## Anti-Patterns Gallery

### Anti-Pattern 1: The Buzzword Storm

> "Our scalable, end-to-end solution leverages cutting-edge AI to empower stakeholders with a holistic approach to next-generation learning."

**What's wrong**: Almost every word is on the banned list. Zero personality. Could be any EdTech company.

---

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Fear Sell

> "The future of education won't wait. Your competitors are already using AI. Don't get left behind — schedule a demo before it's too late."

**What's wrong**: Three fear-based phrases in four sentences. Creates anxiety, not inspiration. Contradicts A2R's opportunity-driven urgency.

---

### Anti-Pattern 3: The Individual Voice

> "As our CEO, Maria, always says: 'The future belongs to those who innovate.' I personally believe that our team has built something special."

**What's wrong**: Breaks collective "we" with individual voices. "I personally believe" is never A2R voice. Individual attribution weakens the collective.

---

### Anti-Pattern 4: The Over-Personified AI

> "Our AI cares deeply about your students' wellbeing and wants to help them succeed. It feels the frustration when a learner struggles and celebrates when they break through."

**What's wrong**: AI personification pushed too far — attributing emotions (caring, feeling, celebrating) crosses from deliberate agency into fake anthropomorphism. A2R personifies with agency verbs (listens, adapts, learns), not emotional states.

---

### Anti-Pattern 5: The Feature Dump

> "A2R offers: adaptive content delivery, real-time analytics, LMS integration, SCORM compliance, multi-language support, custom branding, API access, mobile optimization, and offline mode."

**What's wrong**: Feature list without narrative. No connection to learner outcomes. No personality. Reads like a spec sheet, not A2R.

---

### Anti-Pattern 6: The Softened Spanish

> "Queremos humildemente ofrecer nuestra pequeña contribución al mundo de la educación, con la esperanza de que, quizás, pueda ser de alguna utilidad."

**What's wrong**: Over-hedged, overly humble, no rebel edge. The English would never say "We humbly wish to offer our small contribution." Spanish localization must preserve A2R's confidence and boldness.

## Pattern: The A2R Opening Move

Across all high-visibility content, A2R's most effective opening follows this pattern:

1. **Rebel claim** (1 sentence): Bold, provocative, challenges the status quo
2. **Expert grounding** (1-2 sentences): Evidence, experience, or concrete proof
3. **Forward pull** (1 sentence): Points toward the vision or the "so what"

> "Education hasn't evolved in decades. **[Rebel]** We've spent fifteen years proving it can. **[Expert]** And we're just getting started. **[Forward pull]**"

This pattern scales to headlines (compressed), proposals (expanded with evidence), and even social posts (casual version). The ratio shifts, but the shape stays.
